General Questions about White Glove Consulting Services

How can White Glove Consulting assist my loved one to get approved for Medicaid when he/she has a substantial amount of money in assets and resources?

We have several solutions to help assist you receive the maximum medicaid coverage. The Surplus Program and Pooled Income Trust are designed exactly for this problem, and we can also advise you on how to get other costs covered, such as pharmaceutical drugs and Durable Medical Equipment.

Can my loved one still be eligible if they own a home?

Yes, of course! There are equity limits depending on the state the home is in, but you must either make this their residence or have an "intent to return home" if they are in a nursing home.

Will my loved ones income prevent them from getting approved for Medicaid?

Not necessarily. Depending on their monthly income, we would advise to still apply for one of the Medicaid programs or to learn more about the excess income options.

Is it true that Medicaid looks back at past transfers/transactions in the application process?

The national norm is that the "look-back" period is around 60 months, but there are many states that do not follow this rule. NY state in particular only puts this in practice for recipients of home care. However, to get the exact details of what situations are approved, we recommend you making a consultation with a consultant at White Glove.

How long will it take for the Medicaid application to get approved?

It takes 30-45 days for a community Medicaid application to get approved, however, if your loved one is in need of home care, it is possible to begin receiving home care services before the application gets approved based on our relationships with the agencies.
