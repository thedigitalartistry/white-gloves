Medicare Savings Program

State Medicare Savings Programs can help with other costs associated with healthcare, such as premiums, deductibles, coinsurance, copayments, prescription drug coverage costs. Many people think they are not eligible for these plans, but there are 4 different types of Medicare Savings Programs.

  • Qualified Medicare Beneficiary (QMB) Program

  • Specified Low-Income Medicare Beneficiary (SLMB) Program

  • Qualifying Individual (QI) Program

  • Qualified Disabled and Working Individuals (QDWI) Program

There are different income and resource limits to quality for each program, and they change year to year. However it is recommended to apply even if your income is slightly over the yearly limit. We will advise you on the most advantageous program(s) to apply for and the likelihood of getting approved. It helps to apply for these programs to receive added supplement to your healthcare costs becuase not only do they add up quickly, but it also ensures you are not overpaying for things like pharmaceutical drugs.

Medicaid Planning

We will double check whether you are missing out.

If you have resources that exceed the Medicaid eligibility limits, we will guide you on exactly what has to be done in order to get approved for Medicaid, while protecting your assets. If your monthly income exceeds the Medicaid allowable we will offer to enroll you in the below programs which will entitle you to receive Medicaid, even though you're over the monthly income limit.

Surplus Program

The Surplus Program, also referred to as the "Spend-down Program" or "Medicaid Excess Income Program" offers the ability to get some medical bills covered, even if you are over the monthly resource/income limit. This program is treated like a deductible where if you can show that your monthly medical bills reach your excess income, you can get parts of your medical bills covered. This includes things like outpatient care, doctor and dental visits, lab tests, prescription drugs, and even home care.

Pooled Income Trust Program

Due to strict income limits that affect your medicaid process, a pooled income trust is a great solution for people to ensure their bills are paid while still having medicaid assist with healthcare needs. Instead of paying the surplus to keep your Medicaid active you will be able to deposit the money into the trust account every month, and the trust organization will then utilize your money by paying your monthly bills with it.

Process

At the time of the consultation we will sit with you and go through the entire application process and answer any and all questions you might have.

1

Phone consulting

An initial client phone call is fielded by a trained White Glove Consulting representative who will briefly assess the situation, discuss financials in regard to Medicaid eligibility, and determine the client's needs.

2

Documentation

A White Glove Consulting representative will provide a Medicaid Document Checklist for the client to begin gathering documentation; a face to face consultation will then be scheduled to discuss the Medicaid process in greater detail.

3

1:1 Detail Consultation

A White Glove Consulting representative will meet with the client to discuss their income and assets in full detail, compile information, gather documentation, guide the client on what needs to be done and explain Surplus or Pooled Income Trust programs, if necessary.

4

Paperwork & Review

A White Glove Consulting representative scrutinizes paperwork to ensure the enrollment will not be denied; application is completed entirely, undergoes a final review, and is submitted directly to HRA for processing; application processing time will be expedited whenever required.

5

Pending Application

The Medicaid pending application and paperwork is submitted to the designated home care agency and White Glove Consulting works with the client to obtain required services as soon as possible.

6

Additional

Upon acceptance of the Medicaid application, the Pooled Income Trust package is submitted to the HRA Surplus unit for processing whenever applicable; the client can begin utilizing Medicaid for all future purposes. We also can help with your yearly medicaid renewals.
